Learning Contract PDF:

    Overall, my Guided Learning journey pushed me out of my comfort zone as I had no prior experience in coding or website development. I started by learning the basics of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript through online platforms such as LinkedIn Learning, and applied what I learnt through hands-on practice and mini projects like the Terrarium and Calculator.

    Along the way, I faced challenges such as debugging errors and balancing my time with other academic commitments, especially during busy periods in the semester. However, these challenges helped me develop resilience, adaptability, and better time management. By the end of the project, I was able to independently build and refine my own portfolio website, which gave me greater confidence as a self-directed learner and showed how much I had progressed from the start of my Guided Learning journey.

Plan: Weeks 1 & 2

  • I focused on planning and preparation for my learning project, as I had no prior experience in coding or website-building.
  • I then had the first consultation with Ms Hu, and she advised me on the appropriate sites to use such as finding a suitable course on LinkedIn Learning.
  • I researched suitable online coding tutorials and finalised the platforms I would be using, mainly LinkedIn Learning, alongside sites like W3Schools for specific codes assistance, and forums like StackOverflow for solutions
  • I installed essential tools such as Visual Studio Code and set up the necessary programming environment on GitHub.
  • I refined my Student-Initiated Learning Project Proposal, clarified doubts with my learning facilitator, and finalised my learning plan and action timeline.
  • I also began learning basic HTML, which marked my first hands-on exposure to coding.
  • There was a lot of stuff happening as the semester had just started. I realised that a good handling of time management goes a long way and helps to plan out my tasks to be done, rather than completing it at the last minute.

Perform: Weeks 3 & 4

  • I focused on making steady progress in HTML and understanding how webpages are structured.
  • I learnt how to use basic HTML elements such as headings, paragraphs, images, and links to create simple webpages.
  • Once I've completed the HTML modules of the course, I started out with the CSS modules.
  • I felt motivated at this point to learn the basics of HTML and to set-up a simple Visual Studio Code environment. It was satisfying to accomplish the first milestone of the project.

Perform: Weeks 5 & 6

  • I continued working on CSS and began experimenting with different design elements.
  • I applied colours, spacing, font styles, and layout techniques to improve the overall look and readability of my webpages.
  • I also started working on a Google Sites page to map out the sections that my final website would contain, in preparation for the upcoming consultation.
  • Throughout the two weeks, I was able to complete the tasks as stated in the Learning Contract. However, as the semester progressed and other projects emerge, I knew that I would have to juggle between that and Guided Learning well.

Perform: Weeks 7 & 8

  • From the second consultation with Ms Hu, I had shown her my current progress on HTML and CSS. And demonstrated the current project of the Terrarium mini project.
  • I focused mainly on JavaScript, learning how to handle user interactions and basic logic. Specifically adding animation to the page when loading, and dragging element of a defined item id.
  • The first mini project of the Terrarium was formally done.
  • I then created a separate respository to test out and play around with the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript codes to explore more on the terrarium and the expected results if I tweaked certain codes around.
  • That respository would also be the final E-Portfolio site to host my codes.
  • The feeling of accomplishment hit me as I completed the HTML and CSS modules of the LinkedIn Learning course. However, it was also draining as despite a video walkthrough being available, I still faced issues with my codes that slowed my progress at times. Specifically not linking my CSS file under the same folder as the HTML files. It was a silly mistake but I managed to learn and remember it quickly.

Monitor: Weeks 9 & 10

  • Weeks 9 and 10 coincided with the term test and term break, so progress was lighter (and I must admit, I did not make much progress as I had to focus on my MST).
  • However, once my MST had ended, I reflected on my learning progress and prepare mentally for the final stages of the project as it would involve of a online research (e.g. Experimenting with different CSS codes from W3Schools and StackOverflow for different layouts of the top nav for example).
  • I also thought of proceeding with another mini project to deepen my understanding on JavaScript. Eventually, I decided on making a calculator that can function with an input and output.
  • Although I did not make much progress during these two weeks, I felt that the break was necessary to recharge and prepare myself for the final push towards completing my e-portfolio website. I was determined to make significant progress in the upcoming weeks.

Monitor: Weeks 11 & 12

  • I focused on finalising the draft website and transitioning into building the final version.
  • I refined the website structure, improved navigation, and ensured consistency in design across all pages.
  • I looked up several sites to look for a suitable colour scheme for my final site. I then decided to go ahead with a minimalist scheme of grey, black and white.
  • I ensured that all pages were functional, visually consistent, and easy to navigate. Particularly adding the hyperlinks on the top nav and on the individual HTML pages to navigate.
  • Photos and video recordings to showcase my progress were also compiled and uploading onto VSC for the initial code, and onto GitHub Repository for the final website.
  • During these weeks, I felt a mix of excitement and pressure as I worked to finalise my website. There were moments of frustration when things didn't work as expected, but overcoming those challenges made the process rewarding. I was proud of the progress I had made and eager to see the final product come together.

Reflect: Weeks 13 & 14

  • Concentrated on completing the final website and curating my portfolio content.
  • Had the third consultation and showed Ms Hu my final website. Most of my pages followed through the rubris apart from my reflection that was not done.
  • After finalising and completing my final website, I had the last consultation (TO ADD ON)
  • In these final weeks, I felt a sense of satisfaction as I wrapped up my project. There was a lot of attention to detail required to ensure everything was polished and professional. I learnt the importance of resilience and adaptability, as I had to make last-minute adjustments and improvements. Even up till learning on inserting a PDF as that required a separate folder "pdfs". Overall, it was a fulfilling experience that enhanced my skills and confidence in web development.
